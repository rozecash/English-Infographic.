Thesis Statement

In "Look Both Ways," Jason Reynolds presents the idea that friendship and community provide the essential strength and perspective needed to navigate life's daily conflicts and uncertainties.

Narrative Evidence

Key moments that illustrate the thesis

Water Booger Bears

Chapter 1: Jasmine & TJ

Jasmine is self-conscious and dealing with the "hardship" of classmates mocking her parents. Her strong, established friendship with TJ acts as a safe space. He "never once" makes fun of her, and their shared secret of being "Water Booger Bears" symbolizes a bond that protects them from the judgment of others. Their friendship gives them the strength to be themselves.

The Low Cuts Strike Again

Chapter 4: The Low Cuts

This chapter initially presents a conflict: the kids are perceived as thieves who "strike again" to steal Bit's mom's change. The twist reveals their actions are actually an act of community. They are secretly collecting money to help Bit's mom, who has cancer. Their "conflict" with the world is actually an act of loyalty and support for one of their own.

Call of Duty

Chapter 5: Bryson & Ty

Bryson is ready to fight anyone who bothers him, a constant conflict. This is put to the test when a kid insults his friend Ty. Bryson steps in and fights the bully, getting in trouble as a result. The story shows that his friendship with Ty is more important than his own desire to stay out of trouble. Their bond is tested by a conflict, and it proves strong enough to face it directly.

Conclusion

Through these interconnected stories, Reynolds illustrates that while conflict is inevitable, the bonds of friendship and community provide the resilience necessary to endure it. Whether it is sharing a silly secret name, hustling for loose change, or throwing a punch, these characters prove they are stronger together.
